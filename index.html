How Brainstorm Works

Core Principles

Innovation Focus: Brainstorm must always retain capacity to drive new projects and priorities.

Long-Term Ownership: Mature products transition to non-Brainstorm teams for sustained ownership.

Brainstorm will likely retain AI capability ownership in the short-to-medium term, with potential to shift ownership to where it makes sense long-term.

E.g. document parsing/OCR with the VCR team

Guardrails for Balance

Platform investment is intentional and measured; architect feedback (e.g., Adam and Phil) serves as the barometer.

Maintain at minimum a 20-person dedicated AI innovation investment, regardless of production/platform demands — a growing portfolio of systems we have built cannot slow us down.

Brainstorm Innovation squads operate with speed, minimal overhead, and limited bureaucracy.

Organizational Model

Fluid Team Structure

30 permanent engineers organized into ephemeral, project-focused Squads.

Squads spin up/down quickly, shifting capacity as priorities evolve.

Majority of squads focus on greenfield and net-new innovation.

Innovation Squads

Mission: Take ideas and move them from demo → prototype → proof of concept → pilot → wide release as quickly as possible.

Operating Principles:

Deliver production-ready value.

Maintain security and compliance as non-negotiables.

Operate with flexibility compared to traditional Software Engineering standards, without compromising safety.

Team members shift fluidly; the priority is placing the right person in the right role rather than reporting lines.

Prioritization driven by Product Strategy.

ESO (Endurance, Stability & Offloading) Squad

Mission: Absorb ongoing architectural, platform, and engineering needs without derailing innovation squads.

Flexes up/down with demand.

Backlog Prioritization requires alignment between Adam Lewis, Zoe, Ahmad, Kasey, and SEMs/POs.

Focus Areas:

Platformize products. Address architectural improvements. Deliver quality-of-life & engineering enhancements. Support data platform needs. Expand QA & test automation. Repackage, handoff, & transfer ownership. Drive continuous improvement, monitoring, support, & maintenance.

Data Engineering

Brainstorm includes 3 permanent Data Engineers to support organizational data needs.

Embed with Innovation Squads for data-intensive projects.

Shift to ESO backlog when demand is low, tackling items generated by Architecture, PS, or Engineering.

Leadership Roles

Software Engineering Lead (Applied AI & Cloud): Guides product delivery, aligns with Architecture, and bridges Product Strategy to working software.

Software Engineering Managers (SEMs): Support people and projects, not reporting lines.

Implications

Brainstorm combines the agility of a startup with the discipline of a well-run technology company.

Innovation capacity remains protected and prioritized.

Decision-making remains nimble and scalable as responsibilities evolve.
